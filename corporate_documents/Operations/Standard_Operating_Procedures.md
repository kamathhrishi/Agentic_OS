# Standard Operating Procedures

## Document Control
- **Version:** 2.0
- **Last Updated:** November 2024
- **Owner:** Operations Department
- **Review Cycle:** Quarterly

## Purpose
This document outlines standard operating procedures for key business processes to ensure consistency, efficiency, and compliance.

## Customer Onboarding
### Process
1. Contract signed
2. Account created in CRM
3. Kickoff meeting scheduled (within 48 hours)
4. Technical requirements gathering
5. Implementation plan created
6. Project kickoff
7. Regular check-ins scheduled

### Timeline
- Standard: 30-45 days
- Enterprise: 60-90 days

### Responsible Parties
- Sales: Handoff
- Customer Success: Lead
- Engineering: Technical setup
- Support: Access provisioning

## Incident Management
### Severity Levels
- **P1 - Critical:** System down, business impact
- **P2 - High:** Major feature unavailable
- **P3 - Medium:** Minor feature issue
- **P4 - Low:** Cosmetic or minor issue

### Response Times
- P1: 15 minutes
- P2: 1 hour
- P3: 4 hours
- P4: 24 hours

### Escalation
1. Level 1: Support team
2. Level 2: Engineering team
3. Level 3: Senior engineering/CTO

## Change Management
### Process
1. Change request submitted
2. Impact assessment
3. Approval from change board
4. Implementation plan
5. Testing
6. Deployment
7. Post-deployment review

### Approval Authority
- Minor changes: Team lead
- Medium changes: Department head
- Major changes: Change board

## Vendor Management
### Vendor Selection
1. Requirements definition
2. Vendor research and RFP
3. Evaluation and scoring
4. Reference checks
5. Contract negotiation
6. Approval process

### Vendor Relationship
- Quarterly business reviews
- Annual performance evaluation
- Contract renewals: 90 days before expiry

## Quality Assurance
### Testing Process
1. Unit testing (development)
2. Integration testing
3. QA testing
4. User acceptance testing
5. Production validation

### Release Criteria
- All tests passing
- Documentation updated
- Stakeholder approval
- Rollback plan in place

## Data Security
### Access Control
- Role-based permissions
- Regular access reviews
- Multi-factor authentication required
- Principle of least privilege

### Data Handling
- Encryption at rest and in transit
- Regular backups
- Data retention policies
- Privacy compliance (GDPR, CCPA)
